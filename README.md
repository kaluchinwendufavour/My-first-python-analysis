# My-first-python-analysis
Python data analysis project using jupyter notebook

 🎬 My Netflix Data Story

## 📖 Overview

I opened the Netflix dataset expecting nothing more than a list of movies and TV shows. My goal was simply to practice Python, create a few visualizations, and improve my data analysis skills.

Instead, I discovered that this dataset tells the story of how Netflix transformed from a movie streaming service into a global entertainment powerhouse.

Through data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and visualization, I uncovered patterns that explain Netflix's content strategy and how our viewing habits have evolved over time.

---

## 📊 Dataset

The dataset contains **7,789 Netflix titles** with information including:

- Title
- Director
- Cast
- Country
- Release Year
- Date Added
- Rating
- Duration
- Genre (Listed In)

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- Python
- Jupyter Notebook
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib

---

## 🔍 Analysis Process

The project involved:

- Cleaning missing values
- Converting date columns
- Exploring movies vs TV shows
- Analyzing release year trends
- Examining country distribution
- Investigating content ratings
- Comparing movie durations with TV show seasons
- Creating visualizations to support insights

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 🎥 Movies Still Dominate

Although Netflix is famous for its original series, movies still make up the majority of its catalog. This reflects Netflix's origins as a digital replacement for DVD rentals.

### 🚀 Massive Growth After 2017

The number of titles increased dramatically between **2017 and 2020**, showing Netflix's shift from licensing content to producing its own original programming.

### 🌍 A Truly Global Platform

While I expected the United States to dominate the catalog, countries such as **India**, **the United Kingdom**, and **South Korea** contributed significant amounts of content.

This highlights Netflix's strategy of bringing stories from around the world to a global audience.

### 👥 Adult Audiences Lead

The most common ratings were **TV-MA** and **TV-14**, indicating that Netflix primarily serves teenagers and adults rather than children.

### 📺 Series Drive Retention

Movies usually last between **90–120 minutes**, while TV shows span multiple seasons.

This reinforces the idea that Netflix uses series not only to entertain viewers but also to encourage long-term subscriptions.

This project taught me that data tells stories beyond numbers.

What initially appeared to be a simple catalog of movies and TV shows revealed Netflix's business strategy:
